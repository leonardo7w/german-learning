Prepositions & Their Cases

Every preposition forces a case. Learn which — and crack the two-way Wohin?→Akk / Wo?→Dat switch that's behind most case slips.

Mission link. In real Zurich life — directions, your commute, where things are at work — prepositions come up constantly, and each one dictates the case. Get the three groups automatic and your case errors drop sharply. ~20–25 min, varied verbs throughout. No warm-up this time (those start next lesson).

iThree groups

A preposition belongs to exactly one of these. The first two are pure memorisation — drill them until reflexive.

ALWAYS AKKUSATIV
durch · für · gegen · ohne · um
(+ bis, entlang, wider) — Memory hook: "DOGFU" durch-ohne-gegen-für-um.
ALWAYS DATIV
aus · außer · bei · mit · nach · seit · von · zu · gegenüber
These are extremely common (mit, nach, bei, zu, von…) — worth overlearning.
TWO-WAY (Akk or Dat)
an · auf · hinter · in · neben · über · unter · vor · zwischen
The 9 "Wechselpräpositionen" — the case depends on the verb (next box).

There are also genitive prepositions (während, wegen, trotz, statt, innerhalb…) — a B2 add-on we'll touch later.

iiThe two-way switch

Wohin? (movement into a place) → AKKUSATIV
Wo? (static position in a place) → DATIV

The trick isn't "does anything move" — it's whether the action crosses a boundary into a new location (Akk) or happens within one (Dat). German even has dedicated verb pairs for this:

Wohin? → AKK (put)Wo? → DAT (be)
legen – lay downliegen – be lying
stellen – stand up/placestehen – be standing
setzen – set downsitzen – be sitting
hängen (reg.) – hang uphängen (str.) – be hanging
gehen, fahren, springen… – go/move tosein, bleiben, arbeiten, warten… – stay/be

Ich lege den Laptop auf den Tisch. (Wohin? → Akk)
Der Laptop liegt auf dem Tisch. (Wo? → Dat)

Quick contractions you'll hear constantly: in dem → im, in das → ins, an dem → am, an das → ans, zu dem → zum, zu der → zur, bei dem → beim. Full lists in reference/0003-prepositions-cheatsheet.html.

AWhich group?

Classify each preposition.

BTwo-way: Akkusativ or Dativ?

Read the verb. Is it Wohin? (into → Akk) or Wo? (within → Dat)?

CChoose the form after the preposition

Now commit to the actual article. Watch the group and the gender.

DSpot the error

Correct or wrong? The classic trap: a "Wo?" verb (liegen/stehen/sein) paired with accusative.

EBonus: verbs that own a preposition

Some verbs come welded to a preposition + fixed case — you just memorise the pair (a big B2 vocabulary lever, and a varied-verb workout). A taste:

Verb + prepositionCase
warten auf – wait forAkkusativ
denken an – think ofAkkusativ
sich interessieren fürAkkusativ
teilnehmen an – take part inDativ
gehören zu – belong toDativ
sich kümmern um – take care ofAkkusativ

Here the case is fixed by the verb, not by Wohin/Wo. Ask me for a full set drilled to your field.
