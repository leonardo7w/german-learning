German Learning Resources

Curated, high-trust sources for reaching B2/C1 with solid foundations. Lesson knowledge is drawn from here, not from memory. (Source of truth: RESOURCES.md.)

Knowledge

Deutsche Welle — Learn German
Free, public-broadcaster German courses A1–C1 with audio, video, grammar and exercises. Use for: structured grammar, listening (Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten, Video-Thema B2, Alltagsdeutsch C1), and contemporary vocabulary in context.
Lingolia — German Grammar
Clean, reliable grammar reference with explanations + exercises and a verb conjugator. Use for: looking up a rule quickly (cases, tenses, word order) and drilling it.
Goethe-Institut — Practise German for free
The official German cultural institute: apps, podcasts, games, and 260+ graded exercises. Use for: authoritative, exam-aligned practice and self-assessment of level.
German.net — Cases exercises
Targeted case drills (nominative/accusative/dative/genitive). Use for: extra repetition on the case system between lessons.

Vocabulary & games (spaced repetition)

In-workspace mini-games
Der/Die/Das (gender), Verb Lab, Verbs + Prepositions — Leitner spaced repetition, progress saved in-browser. Use for: building vocabulary between lessons.
Anki
Free, powerful spaced-repetition flashcards. Use for: long-term vocabulary retention.
Quizlet
Flashcards with game-like study modes (match, test). Use for: quick gamified drilling.
Gender-by-suffix rules
Use for: predicting der/die/das from endings (-ung→die, -chen→das, -ling→der…).
Lingoda — Verbs with prepositions
Use for: the canonical B1–B2 verb + fixed-preposition + case pairs.

Wisdom (Communities)

r/German
Large, well-moderated subreddit. Use for: grammar questions, sanity-checking sentences, and "does this sound natural?" feedback from natives and advanced learners.
Tandem / HelloTalk
Language-exchange apps to pair with native speakers. Use for: real conversation practice toward the living/working-abroad goal — the single best way to build fluency.
Local Volkshochschule (VHS) or a Stammtisch / language meetup
Use for: in-person, real-time speaking practice. (Zurich-specific options to be added.)

Swiss context

ch.ch — The Swiss Authorities Online
Official portal explaining Swiss administrative processes (permits, insurance, taxes). Use for: real Behörden vocabulary and understanding the bureaucracy you'll handle in German.
Note on Swiss German: in Zurich the spoken dialect is Züritüütsch, but Hochdeutsch is the written/formal standard and the basis of B2/C1. We target Hochdeutsch; Swiss-German listening is a later add-on.
