# German Grammar Glossary

Canonical terminology for this workspace. Terms are added only once the user can use them
correctly. All lessons should adhere to these definitions.

## Cases

**Case (Kasus)**:
The grammatical "job" a noun plays in a sentence. German has four, and the case changes the
article (and sometimes the noun/adjective ending) rather than the word order.
_Avoid_: declension (that's the act of changing the forms, not the case itself).

**Nominative (Nominativ)**:
The case of the subject — who or what does the action. Test question: **wer/was?**
_Avoid_: "the basic form" (every case is a real form).

**Accusative (Akkusativ)**:
The case of the direct object — the thing directly acted upon. Test question: **wen/was?**
_Avoid_: "the object case" (dative is also an object case).

**Dative (Dativ)**:
The case of the indirect object — the recipient/beneficiary. Test question: **wem?**
_Avoid_: "the giving case" (a useful hint, not a definition).

**Genitive (Genitiv)**:
The case of possession/relation — "of the…". Test question: **wessen?**
_Avoid_: "the possessive" (English possessive ≠ German genitive scope).

**Definite article (bestimmter Artikel)**:
der/die/das and their case forms — points to a specific, known noun ("the").

**Indefinite article (unbestimmter Artikel)**:
ein/eine and their case forms — points to a non-specific noun ("a/an").
