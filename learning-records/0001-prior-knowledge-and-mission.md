# Established: intermediate level, shaky foundations, B2/C1 goal for living abroad

The learner self-identifies as **intermediate** in German with foundations that feel shaky,
and wants to reach **B2 then C1** in order to **live and work** in a German-speaking country.

In the opening interview they flagged weak spots across the board — cases & endings, verb
tenses, word order, vocabulary/fluency, and topic-specific vocabulary — and chose **cases &
endings** as the first to tackle. They want **highly interactive** lessons (rules + drills +
examples + scenarios + spot-the-error + games/quizzes/flashcards), at **20–30 min** each.

## Implications
- Don't re-teach absolute beginner material; the job is **reinforcement and precision**, not
  first exposure. Calibrate quizzes above A1 — assume vocabulary is known, target accuracy.
- Start from the **case system** as the keystone, then build outward (adjective endings →
  prepositions → word order → tenses → fluency/vocab). See NOTES.md sequence.
- Every lesson must include an interactive, auto-feedback component — text-only won't land.
