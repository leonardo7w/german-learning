# Prepositions solid (27/28); two-way Wohin/Wo decision reliable

The learner scored **27/28** on Lesson 0004 (prepositions), including the two-way
Wechselpräpositionen with the Wohin?→Akk / Wo?→Dat switch and verb+fixed-preposition items.

## Implications
- **Prepositions are no longer a case-error source.** Since prepositions were a major driver of the
  learner's residual case mistakes (per LR-0003), this meaningfully shrinks the "cases still shaky"
  problem — the remaining risk is dative-verbs and bare case ID, not preposition-driven case.
- The case **warm-ups** in future lessons can de-emphasise preposition items and lean toward
  dative-verbs (helfen/danken/folgen/gefallen) and plain subject/object/recipient ID.
- Confidence is high enough to keep the difficulty up. Lesson 06 (Perfekt vs Präteritum) can move at
  pace; no need to slow down for prepositions.
