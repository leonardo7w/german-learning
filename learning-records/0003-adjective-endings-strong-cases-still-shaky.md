# Adjective endings mastered (38/40); cases still shaky; lessons running long

After Lesson 0002, the learner scored **38/40** on adjective endings including the harder
preposition-driven and B2 scenario items — strong, near-mastery of weak/mixed/strong declension.
However they report **still mixing up the underlying cases**, and that the lesson **took 40+ minutes**
(target is 20–30).

## Evidence
- Self-reported 38/40 on Lesson 0002 (which spans pattern-ID, per-declension drills, preposition
  boss-level, spot-the-error, and a B2 scenario).

## Implications
- **Cap lesson length.** Aim for 20–30 min: fewer exercises per lesson (~15–20, not 40), or split
  big topics across two lessons. Lesson 0002 was too long.
- **Adjective endings are no longer the bottleneck — cases are.** The endings are correct *as long as
  the case is correct*; the remaining errors trace back to case selection, especially dative-verbs
  (helfen/danken/folgen/gefallen) and prepositions.
- **Build spaced repetition for cases.** Per the learner's request, include a few case (and some
  ending) review items "every now and then." Convention going forward: each new lesson opens with a
  short 3–4 question case warm-up, and there is a standalone repeatable mixed-review drill
  (lessons/0003) they can run anytime.
