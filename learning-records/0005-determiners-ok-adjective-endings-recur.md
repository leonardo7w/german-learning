# Determiners understood (19/26); adjective-ending recall still slips under load

The learner scored **19/26** on Lesson 0005 (determiners). The der-word/ein-word concept landed,
but the misses clustered on **adjective endings after determiners** (the combo items) — i.e. the
ending recall degrades when stacked on top of another decision, even though endings were 38/40 in
isolation (LR-0003).

## Implications
- **Adjective endings are not "done."** They're correct in isolation but fragile under combined load
  (determiner + case + ending at once). Keep recycling them: warm-ups and the mixed-review drill
  should include determiner+adjective combo items, not just bare endings.
- This is exactly the "keep including these every now and then" the learner asked for — honour it.
- Next teaching topic (verb tenses) is a fresh system, which is fine; but every lesson's opening
  warm-up should keep a steady trickle of case/ending/determiner recall.
