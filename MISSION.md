# Mission: German for living and working abroad (B2 → C1)

## Why
I'm at an intermediate level but my foundations are shaky, which holds me back from
expressing myself confidently. I want to live and work in a German-speaking country,
so I need German that is accurate enough to be taken seriously professionally and
fluid enough to handle everyday life — ultimately operating comfortably at B2, then C1.

## Success looks like
- I use the four cases and correct articles/endings automatically, without freezing mid-sentence.
- I form complex sentences (subordinate clauses, correct verb position) under real-time pressure.
- I handle workplace and bureaucratic situations in German — Termine, emails, Behörden, meetings.
- I have topic-specific vocabulary for the domains I actually live and work in.
- I can sustain a natural conversation that *sounds* German rather than translated English.

## Constraints
- ~20–30 minutes per lesson.
- Already intermediate: don't re-teach absolute basics; reinforce and sharpen instead.
- Learns best through a *mix*: clear rules + drills, lots of examples, scenarios/conversation,
  spot-the-error, and interactive games/quizzes/flashcards. Keep lessons interactive.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Full literary/academic register and exam-cramming for a specific certificate (revisit if an
  exam date appears).
- Regional dialects (Bairisch, Schwyzerdütsch, etc.) — focus on Standard German first.
