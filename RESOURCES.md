# German Learning Resources

Curated, high-trust sources for reaching B2/C1 with solid foundations. Knowledge in
lessons is drawn from here, not from memory.

## Knowledge

- [Deutsche Welle — Learn German](https://learngerman.dw.com/en/overview)
  Free, public-broadcaster German courses A1–C1 with audio, video, grammar and exercises.
  Use for: structured grammar, listening (Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten, Video-Thema B2,
  Alltagsdeutsch C1), and contemporary vocabulary in context.
- [Lingolia — German Grammar](https://deutsch.lingolia.com/en/grammar)
  Clean, reliable grammar reference with explanations + accompanying exercises and a verb
  conjugator. Use for: looking up a rule quickly (cases, tenses, word order) and drilling it.
- [Goethe-Institut — Practise German for free](https://www.goethe.de/en/spr/ueb.html)
  The official German cultural institute: apps, podcasts, games, and 260+ graded exercises.
  Use for: authoritative, exam-aligned practice and self-assessment of level.
- [German.net — Cases exercises](https://german.net/exercises/cases/)
  Targeted case drills (nominative/accusative/dative/genitive). Use for: extra repetition
  on the case system between lessons.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/German](https://www.reddit.com/r/German/)
  Large, well-moderated subreddit. Use for: grammar questions, sanity-checking sentences,
  and "does this sound natural?" feedback from natives and advanced learners.
- [Tandem](https://www.tandem.net/) / [HelloTalk](https://www.hellotalk.com/)
  Language-exchange apps to pair with native speakers. Use for: real conversation practice
  toward the living/working-abroad goal — the single best way to build fluency.
- Local Volkshochschule (VHS) or a Stammtisch / language meetup once abroad.
  Use for: in-person, real-time speaking practice. (To be added once location is known.)
