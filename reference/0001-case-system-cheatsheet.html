German Case System — Cheat Sheet

The four cases, their articles, and how to spot which one to use. Reference 0001.

The four cases at a glance

NOM Nominative

The subject — who/what does the action.
Test: wer? / was?
Der Mann schläft. — The man sleeps.

AKK Accusative

The direct object — what is acted upon.
Test: wen? / was?
Ich sehe den Mann. — I see the man.

DAT Dative

The indirect object — the recipient.
Test: wem?
Ich gebe dem Mann ein Buch. — …to the man.

GEN Genitive

Possession / relation — "of the…".
Test: wessen?
Das Auto des Mannes — the man's car.

The 80/20: Notice how little actually changes. Feminine & neuter & plural barely move across nom/acc. The big action is in the masculine column (der → den → dem → des) and the dative row. Master those two and you've got most of it.

The endings (-en, -em, -er, -es) are identical for ein, kein and the possessives mein, dein, sein, ihr, unser, euer, Ihr. Learn the pattern once, reuse everywhere.

Two extra noun changes (don't forget these)

  • Dative plural: the noun adds -n (unless it already ends in -n or -s). die Kinder → mit den Kindern
  • Genitive masculine/neuter: the noun adds -s or -es. der Mann → des Mannes; das Auto → des Autos

How to pick the case — the procedure

  • 1. Find the verb. Many verbs dictate the case (e.g. helfen, danken, folgen → dative).
  • 2. Find the subject (wer/was does it?) → NOM.
  • 3. Find the direct object (wen/was?) → AKK.
  • 4. Find the recipient (wem?) → DAT.
  • 5. Prepositions override: after a preposition, the preposition (or its meaning) sets the case.
Preposition quick-list (next lesson goes deep): always AKK: durch, für, gegen, ohne, um · always DAT: aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu · two-way (AKK = motion/where to?, DAT = location/where?): in, an, auf, über, unter, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen.
